The word bangle comes from the traditional words Bangri or Bangali, which mean "the ornament that adorns the arm" in Sanskrit. In India, bangles are an integral part of a woman's identity, and represent good luck in marriage, good health, fortune, and success. For example, after marriage, it is customary to wear bangles as a symbol of these things. Some also consider the number of bangles a woman wears to indicate the richness of her family.
